<p>​​​​It is the responsibility of Account Managers​ to book developers for known client work, however anyone can book a developer, including the developer themselves. Also if a developer notices they should be booked, but there's nothing in their calendar, and they can't figure out the below, as a minimum they should ask the Account Manager to book them.​<br></p><p>​To book a developer:​<br></p><dl class="image"><dt>
      <img alt="Create new appointment in CRM" src="ServiceCalendar2013.jpg" style="width:95%;" />
   </dt><dd>Figure: Using the Service Calendar, you can see who is and is not available at a given time</dd></dl><p>There are a few different ways of booking developers for project work, either via Outlook or the browser.​<br></p>
<br><excerpt class='endintro'></excerpt><br>

If you're booking yourself AND someone else, have both of you in the To box.<br></li></ol><li>Invite the client as optional. If you do this, also make it a Teams Meeting and add your email signature<br></li><li>Set the time and location for your appointment​<br></li><li>Set the recurrence if it’s more than 1 day of work<br></li><li>Click "Dynamics 365" on the ribbon which will open a side panel and click on "Set Regarding" then search select the company that you want to book the developers to work on (this is the step that syncs it with CRM)<br></li></ol><dl class="image"><dt> <img src="Dynamics1.jpg" alt="Dynamics1.jpg" style="margin:5px;width:750px;height:299px;" /> <br></dt><dd>Figure: A complete In this case, create a Tentative Booking which reserves the consultant for the period of the booking without assigning t​​h​em to the client account. The purpose of a Tentative Booking is to reserve a consultant and trigger a conversation between Account Ma​nagers if the consultant is required on confirmed client work over the same period.<br></p><p>To create a Tentative Booking:</p><ol><li>Follow the same steps above to create the Appointment​<br></li><li>Instead of choosing a client account for 'Set Regarding', choose the company (E.g. "ssw.pencilledin").</li></ol><p><b>Tip:</b> Create a "Booked In Days" Report and make the Tentative Bookings display obviously (E.g. A grey color), so Account Managers can easily see it​​ if they need to use that time.​<br></p><h3 class="ssw15-rteElement-H3">Adding Internal Bookings<br></h3><p>If a developer is needed for non-billable work (E.g. Urgent internal work), or travelling to teach public training events, their time should be blocked out so it shows as unavailable for client work.<br></p><p>To book someone for internal work:<br></p><ol><li>​​<span style="background-color:initial;color:#444444;">​​Follow the same steps as above to create the Appointment</span><br></li><li><span style="background-color:initial;color:#444444;">Instead of choosing a client account for "Set Regarding", choose your own company (E.g. "SSW").</span></li></ol><div><img src="2020-03-20_10-49-19.png" alt="2020-03-20_10-49-19.png" style="margin:5px;width:650px;" /><br><strong>Figure: Internally Booked days show as black and Pencilled In days show as grey​​​​</strong><br></div><div><strong><br></strong></div><h3 class="ssw15-rteElement-H3">Using a separate Oulook calendar to book appointments<br></h3><p class="ssw15-rteElement-P">​If you have a lot of staff, it can be useful to use a separate calendar to make your bookings to stop them from covering your every day calendar making it hard to read.<br></p><p class="ssw15-rteElement-P"> Unfortunately, you cannot use a sub-folder to track appointments in Outlook.

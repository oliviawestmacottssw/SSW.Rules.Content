<p>​Just as barcodes allow machines to read data from items in stores, QR codes are a barcode-like vector between online and offline information. QR codes can be easily accessed by any phone in the world that has a camera. They enable everything from online to offline (O2O) marketplaces, which are huge in China.<br></p>
<br><excerpt class='endintro'></excerpt><br>
<h3 class="ssw15-rteElement-H3">#1 Use a QR code to follow a WeChat Business/Brand Account<br></h3><p>Customers - WeChat allows users to follow a Business/Brand account upon scanning (use the built-in QR scanner). QR codes aren’t just used online, but also in a store or on business cards to save you typing in a URL.</p><p>Businesses - Followers of the WeChat Business/Brand accounts can be sent a limited number of messages per month, usually product info and promotions. <br></p><dl class="ssw15-rteElement-ImageArea"> <img src="Microsoft official account.GIF" alt="" style="width:770px;" /> </dl><p> 
   <strong>Figure: </strong> <strong>A QR on a Microsoft’s newsletter, it allows customers to scan and follow the business account.<br></strong></p><p>
   <strong><br></strong></p><dl class="ssw15-rteElement-ImageArea"> <img src="Follow official account.png" alt="" style="width:770px;height:385px;" /> </dl><p> 
   <strong>Figure</strong><strong>: The WeChat app can scan the QR code, then allow the customer to click the follow button. They will then receive regular updates.<br>​</strong></p><h3 class="ssw15-rteElement-H3">#2 Use a QR code to share WeChat contact information</h3><p class="ssw15-rteElement-P">All WeChat users get their own customized QR code. It can be shown for anyone else to scan, or shared.</p><p class="ssw15-rteElement-P">Note: QR codes can be scanned by tapping and hold on it on screen. <br></p><dl class="ssw15-rteElement-ImageArea"> <img src="personal qr code.png" alt="" style="width:770px;" />  </dl><p class="ssw15-rteElement-P"> 
   <strong>Figure: Customized WeChat’s personal QR code by change style</strong><br><br></p><dl class="ssw15-rteElement-ImageArea"> <img src="extract a qr code.PNG" alt="" style="width:330px;" /> </dl><p class="ssw15-rteElement-P"> 
   <strong>Figure: Tap and hold on a QR image to extract a QR code</strong><br>​<br></p><h3 class="ssw15-rteElement-H3">#3 Use a QR code to receive money in Alipay <br></h3><p>Alipay allows users to receive funds via QR code without adding the payee as a contact.<br>Users create a QR code for payer to scan and pay.<br></p><dl class="ssw15-rteElement-ImageArea"> <img src="receive money alipay.png" alt="" style="width:660px;height:628px;" /> </dl><p>
   <strong>Figure: How to show your “Pay-me” QR code on Alipay</strong><br><br></p><h3 class="ssw15-rteElement-H3">#4 Use a QR code to make a payment offline by using WeChat and Alipay</h3><p>Making a payment to an offline merchant can be done via QR code.</p><p>Once your total has been rung up, show your “Pay Vendor” QR code for the merchant to scan and pay.<br></p><dl class="ssw15-rteElement-ImageArea"> <img src="pay vendor wechat.png" alt="" style="width:660px;height:545px;" /> </dl><p> 
   <strong>Figure: How to show your “Pay Vendor” QR code on WeChat</strong></p><p>

It is easier than putting a link on ads or asking customers to search in App stores.<br> </p><dl class="ssw15-rteElement-ImageArea"> <img src="install apps qr code.jpeg" alt="" style="width:770px;height:513px;" /> </dl> <strong>Figure: A government-sponsored billboard in Hangzhou showcasing apps for tourists</strong><br><strong> </strong> <br> 
<h3 class="ssw15-rteElement-H3">#7 Use a QR code to sign-up and login websites<br></h3><p>Some websites offer login using WeChat’s Third-party authentication API - simply use WeChat to scan their QR code.<br></p><dl class="ssw15-rteElement-ImageArea"> <img src="zhihu login.png" alt="" style="width:770px;" /> </dl> <strong>Figure: use WeChat to sign-up and login to Zhihu (A Chinese Quora replacement)</strong>
<p>
   <br>
</p><p>Some social networks can also offer login by scanning a QR code using the network’s own app instead of entering a user name and a password.<br></p><dl class="ssw15-rteElement-ImageArea"> <img src="weibo login.png" alt="" style="width:770px;height:361px;" /> </dl> <strong>Figure: use Weibo App to scan a QR code to login their services on the web.</strong><br>​<br>
<p></p><p></p><h3 class="ssw15-rteElement-H3">#8 Use a QR code to connect a hotspot</h3><p>Wi-Fi via WeChat is a feature for businesses and public places offering free Wi-Fi hotspots to their customers. It’s a common service in China. Rather than having to find the publicly posted Wi-Fi password, users can log in quickly using their WeChat accounts.<br></p><dl class="ssw15-rteElement-ImageArea"> <img src="hotspot qr code.jpg" alt="" style="width:770px;height:787px;" /> </dl> <strong>Figure: how to connect to a public hotspot</strong><br>​<br>
<p></p><h3 class="ssw15-rteElement-H3">#9 Use a QR code to ride a bus or subway<br></h3><p>You can use QR codes to ride a bus or subway in most main cities in China. </p><p>For example, to ride a bus using Alipay QR code in Hangzhou:</p><p>You have to apply for a virtual bus card and use the card when you are boarding a bus.<br></p><dl class="ssw15-rteElement-ImageArea"> <img src="ride bus qr code.jpg" alt="" style="width:770px;height:814px;" /> </dl> <strong>Figure: how to ride a bus using the Alipay QR code in Hangzhou</strong><br>  <dl class="ssw15-rteElement-ImageArea"> <img src="show qr code bus.jpg" alt="" /> </dl> <strong>Figure: Presenting your QR code to a bus scanner.</strong><br>​<br>
